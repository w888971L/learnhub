# Mission

LearnHub is not a learning management system.

The Django application is real and functional — it has courses, enrollments, assignments, grading, discussions. But the application is the *substrate*, not the purpose. LearnHub exists to prove and refine a vision:

**Human operators and teams of AI agents, governed by constitutional architecture, can manage arbitrarily large codebases — efficiently, safely, and flexibly.**

The goal is a system where:

- A human operator works at the meta-level — reviewing plans, making decisions, sensing when something is off — without needing to read every line of code
- Multiple AI agents (different models, different strengths) collaborate through shared governance documents, not through direct communication
- The knowledge layer (constitution, charters, cross-cutting concerns) scales independently of the codebase, compressing semantic structure into a compact orientation surface
- Independent enforcers verify that the knowledge layer stays consistent with the code it describes
- The whole system is self-reinforcing: agents consume the Memex to do their work, and maintain the Memex as a consequence of doing their work

The LMS is the proving ground. The constitutional architecture is the product.
